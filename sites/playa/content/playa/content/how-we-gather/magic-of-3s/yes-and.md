## Why We Don't Say No üé≠

In improv theater, there's a golden rule: never say "no."

When your scene partner says "Look, a dragon!" ‚Äî you don't say "That's not a dragon, that's a chair." You say "Yes! And it's breathing fire! Run!"

**"Yes... And!"** builds momentum. It keeps the scene going. It creates something together that neither person could create alone.

We use the same philosophy in our community.

---

## The Promise

When you bring us an idea, a need, or a request ‚Äî you will never hear **just "no."**

Even when we can't help directly, we will:

| Step | What We Do | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Acknowledge** | "Yes, this sounds valuable" | "A community garden is a great idea." |
| **Explain** | Why it's not a fit for us to own | "We don't have the space or expertise for growing food." |
| **Enable** | Here's how you can make it happen | "Here's how to organize it yourself." |
| **Connect** | Here's who might help | "Have you talked to the San Diego Community Garden Network?" |
| **Offer** | What support we *can* provide | "We could promote it through our channels and maybe loan tools." |

**The result:** You leave with something useful, even if it's not what you originally asked for.

## Why This Matters

Burning Man culture is built on **radical inclusion** and **participation**. When someone comes forward with energy and ideas, that's precious. That's heat. üî•

If our response kills that energy ‚Äî if we just say "no" and close the door ‚Äî we've failed our mission. We've let the fire die.

Instead, we try to **redirect energy toward possibility**.

## Real Examples

### "We want to start a weekly fire spinning practice"

> **Acknowledge:** "Yes, that would be amazing for the community."
> 
> **Explain:** "SDCAP can't own/run ongoing programming right now ‚Äî we don't have the capacity."
> 
> **Enable:** "Here's how you can run it yourselves ‚Äî find a park, get permits, bring your own insurance."
> 
> **Connect:** "Talk to [name], they organized something similar last year."
> 
> **Offer:** "We can promote your sessions through our calendar, and we might be able to help with insurance if it grows."

### "I want SDCAP to fund my personal art studio"

> **Acknowledge:** "Yes, personal creative space matters."
> 
> **Explain:** "Our grants fund community-accessible projects, not private spaces."
> 
> **Enable:** "You could apply for a project residency at CoLab, or run a funded workshop series."
> 
> **Connect:** "Here are other grant sources that fund individual artists."
> 
> **Offer:** "If you want to make your studio semi-public (open hours for community), let's talk about how we might support that."

### "Can you run a theme camp for us?"

> **Acknowledge:** "Yes, theme camps are core to our culture."
> 
> **Explain:** "We don't run camps ‚Äî camps are community-led."
> 
> **Enable:** "Here's the guide to starting a camp. Here's how registration works."
> 
> **Connect:** "Here are some established camps that might be looking for members."
> 
> **Offer:** "We can provide info sessions, connect you with experienced camp leads, and help promote your recruiting."

## The Improv Mindset

The key to "Yes... And!" is **building on what's there** instead of shutting it down.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "That's not our thing" | "That's interesting ‚Äî here's who does focus on that" |
| "We can't do that" | "We can't own that, but here's what we *can* do" |
| "No budget for that" | "Not this cycle, but here's how to strengthen your proposal for next time" |
| "That's been tried before" | "We learned some things when we tried that ‚Äî want to hear what we found?" |

## When We Actually Can't Help

Sometimes the answer really is no. Some things genuinely don't align with our mission, capacity, or values.

But even then, we don't just shut the door:

- We **explain clearly** why it's not a fit
- We **wish you well** on finding another path
- We **keep the door open** for future ideas

**"No" to this specific thing doesn't mean "no" to you.**

---

## The Culture We're Building

"Yes... And!" isn't just a policy. It's a way of being.

When someone in the community has an idea ‚Äî even a weird one, even a complicated one ‚Äî our default mode is **curiosity** and **enablement**, not gatekeeping.

- **Curious:** "Tell me more about what you're imagining"
- **Enabling:** "Here's how you might make that happen"
- **Generous:** "What resources can we point you toward?"

This creates a culture where people **bring ideas forward** instead of assuming they'll be rejected.

More ideas ‚Üí more energy ‚Üí more fire. üî•

## What We Ask in Return

The "Yes... And!" promise works both ways:

- If we give you a path forward, **try walking it**
- If we connect you with someone, **actually reach out**
- If we explain why something isn't a fit, **hear us out**
- If your idea doesn't land this time, **don't give up**

We're doing our best. So are you. Let's keep building together.
